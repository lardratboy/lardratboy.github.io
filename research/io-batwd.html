Field manual

esc frees the cursor to scroll · H toggles

Every block is made of blocks. Press E on one to open it: it becomes 8×8×8 smaller blocks of the same stuff. Mine a shaft down into it and scroll to shrink until you fit — you'll drop inside, and it's a whole world. Repeat forever. Scroll the other way to grow back out; the world you left is still there, above you, enormous.

Once a block is open you are looking at its 512 children, so the crosshair grabs them. Press [ to aim at bigger blocks: the open block folds back up into one block you can break, grab or stamp whole. ] aims finer again. The aim is held as a size relative to you, so it follows you down.

G picks up the block you're looking at together with everything inside it — every sub-block, every sub-sub-block, however deep you carved. Right-click stamps it back down. A subtree has no scale, so you can stamp what you built at 10⁶× as a mountain, or stamp it inside itself.

And you can promote it. Press . and the thing in your hand stamps one level up: its 512 children stop being the inside of a block and become 8×8×8 blocks of the world you're standing in, each eight times the size it was, each still carrying whatever was inside it. Press . again for 64³. Carve at the scale of a grain of salt; stamp it out as terrain. , steps back down.

The thing in your hand can be turned: R spins it a quarter-turn, T tips it over toward you, M mirrors it — exactly, all the way down. Every nested world inside turns with it, to any depth, with zero error: on a block grid a quarter-turn is just a re-shelving of integers. U undoes breaks, places and stamps; Y redoes.

And the thing in your hand can be made alive. Press K and it becomes a species with a name. Stamp it out a hundred times — those aren't copies, they're instances. Dive into any one of them and carve: all one hundred change, because you didn't edit a block, you edited the species. X freezes one you're aiming at into a snapshot that stops listening. And a species can be stamped inside itself — infinity is now a thing you can build by hand.

Press F to stand up and walk. Gravity is written in units of you, so a fall from a block feels the same whether the block is a metre wide or a nanometre.

Scriptorium

Blocks all the way down.

A block world you can fall into. Look at a block, press E: it opens, and turns out to be made of 512 smaller blocks of the same stuff.

Mine a tunnel into it, then scroll to shrink. You'll drop inside, and it's a world — with its own stone, its own dirt, its own ore. Do it again. And again.
